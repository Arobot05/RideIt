Gears

Bicycle gears are crucial for adjusting pedaling effort to match different terrains and conditions. The gear system, consisting of chainrings, a cassette, and a chain, allows riders to change the gear ratio by shifting the chain between different-sized rings and sprockets. This enables smooth transitions and efficient pedaling, whether climbing hills or speeding on flat roads. Shifters and derailleurs control this process, ensuring a comfortable and effective ride.

Cassette

A bicycle cassette is a cluster of gears (sprockets) attached to the rear wheel's freehub, allowing the rider to change gears and adjust pedaling effort. When you shift gears using the shifter on the handlebars, the rear derailleur moves the chain across the sprockets on the cassette. This changes the gear ratio, making pedaling easier or harder depending on the terrain. The cassette's design ensures smooth transitions between gears, enhancing the bike's performance and efficiency.
